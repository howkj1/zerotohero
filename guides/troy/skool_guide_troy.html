Setting Up Mindset Over Matter on Skool

This guide walks through building your full Christian life coaching platform on Skool — from creating the account to your first paying member. Follow phases in order. No Skool account is assumed — this starts at the beginning.

Skool: ~$99/month platform fee 2.9% transaction fee on member payments 14-day free trial available ⚠ Verify current pricing at skool.com before purchasing

What Skool Is — and How Your 3 Tiers Map to It

Skool is an all-in-one community platform for coaches and creators. One subscription gives you a community discussion feed, a course/video library, an events calendar, a member directory, and a public landing page — all in one place. Members log in, take your courses, join events, and talk to each other. You manage everything from one dashboard.

Skool is built around a single community with one membership price. It does not natively support multiple price tiers within one group. That changes how your 3-tier model works — and the solution is simple once you understand it.

How to handle your 3-tier model on Skool The recommended approach: run one paid Skool community that covers your Basic and Intermediate tiers combined. Set a single monthly price that reflects the combined value. Your Advanced 1:1 coaching engagement ($150/week × 4 weeks) lives outside Skool — sold via a booking link (Calendly or Cal.com) that you post inside your community. Members who want private coaching click the link, book, and pay directly through your booking tool.

Create one paid Skool group. Set the price at $50/month. This covers course library access and group coaching. Anyone who joins gets both. Sell 1:1 coaching via a Calendly link pinned inside the community. This is the simplest structure — one group to manage, one price to explain, one URL to share. Start here. Add Later When Ready

Option B — Free Entry Group + Paid Community

Create a second, separate Skool group that's free to join. Post some content there (intro videos, free devotionals, community discussion). More to manage, but highly effective for growing your list before asking for a commitment.

This guide builds Option A — the single paid community. Phase 4 and 5 cover adding content and group sessions. At the end, notes on adding a free group later if you choose Option B.

4
Understand what Skool does automatically

Once Stripe is connected and your price is set, Skool handles:

  • Monthly recurring billing (charges members on their join date each month)
  • Failed payment retries and member notifications
  • Automatic cancellation when a member stops paying
  • Stripe receipt emails to members after each charge

You do not need to manually manage subscriptions. Skool and Stripe handle the billing loop entirely.

Understand how Skool Classroom is structured

Skool's Classroom uses a two-level structure:

  • Courses — top-level containers, like folders. Example: "Foundations," "Identity & Purpose," "Group Session Replays."
  • Lessons — individual items inside a course. Each lesson can contain video, text, images, attachments, or a combination.

Members see courses in the Classroom tab. They click a course, then click lessons in order. Progress is tracked automatically.

3
Add video to a lesson — two options

Inside any lesson, click the video icon to add video. Skool supports two approaches:

  • Direct upload — Upload MP4 directly to Skool. Skool hosts and streams it. Best for private content. File size limits apply — check Skool's current limits.
  • YouTube Unlisted embed — Upload the video to YouTube as "Unlisted" (not public, not searchable), then paste the link into the lesson. The video plays inside Skool but is hosted on YouTube's servers. Best for large video files or if you're already using YouTube.
YouTube Unlisted is the recommended approach No file size limits, faster upload, YouTube's reliable playback. "Unlisted" means the video won't appear in search or your YouTube channel — only people with the direct link (or who are inside your Skool lesson) can view it.

6
Set Up 1:1 Private Coaching — Booking & Payment
~30 minutes — uses Calendly (free plan works) + Stripe
Not Started
Why Calendly instead of Skool for this Skool doesn't support tiered pricing within a single community — you can't charge some members more inside Skool. Calendly lets you create a bookable event type with payment collection built in. Members inside your Skool community click a link to your Calendly, book, and pay there. Clean, simple, and professional.
1
Create a Calendly account

Go to calendly.com → Sign up with your Google account. The free plan is sufficient to start. If you want payment collection inside Calendly (so clients pay when they book), you'll need Calendly Standard (~$10/month). Alternatively, you can collect payment via a Stripe payment link and send it manually after booking — either approach works.

2
Create a "4-Week Private Coaching" event type

Inside Calendly, click + New Event Type. Configure it:

  • Name: "4-Week Private Coaching with Troy"
  • Duration: 60 minutes (per session)
  • Location: Google Meet (Calendly auto-generates a Meet link for each booking)
  • Description: "Four weeks of dedicated 1:1 coaching with Troy Smith — one 60-minute private session per week. $150/week · $600 total."
3
Set your available time slots

In Calendly, connect your Google Calendar so it checks for conflicts automatically. Set the days and hours you're available for private sessions. Block off times for group calls, family, and your own prep. You control exactly when clients can book.

4
Handle payment for the 4-week engagement

You have two options for collecting the $150/week payment:

  • Option A — Calendly + Stripe (cleanest): On Calendly Standard (~$10/mo), connect Stripe and add a $150 charge to the booking. Client pays when they book each session. You invoice weekly.
  • Option B — Stripe Payment Link (free): Create a Payment Link at dashboard.stripe.com for $150 labeled "Private Coaching Session." After a client books via Calendly, send them the Stripe link by email to complete payment. More manual but no monthly cost.
  • Option C — Manual invoice: Book via Calendly free, invoice via email, collect payment via Venmo/Zelle/PayPal. Simplest but no automatic receipts.
Recommendation Use Option B (Stripe Payment Link) to start — zero additional monthly cost, professional receipts, and you stay in your existing Stripe account. If volume increases, upgrade to Calendly Standard for Option A.
5
Post your Calendly link inside your Skool community

Once your booking link is ready, add it to two places inside Skool:

  • Pinned Community post — Write a post explaining 1:1 coaching and pin it to the top of the Community tab
  • About section — Add a brief mention and link in your group's About/description page

One video, or one podcast episode, or one thoughtful Community post — every week. Consistency over volume. Members who see regular content additions stay subscribed. Members who see nothing cancel. Pick a day of the week and make it your content day. Every week. That's the whole content strategy.
